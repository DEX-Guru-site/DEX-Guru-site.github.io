Pool Analytics: Reading Liquidity Depth, Volume Quality, and LP Concentration

The liquidity pool behind a token is the infrastructure that makes trading possible. Understanding pool health is essential for assessing both trading cost and exit risk.

Liquidity depth

How much capital is locked in the pool's two-sided position. Deeper liquidity means lower slippage for your trade. A pool with $50K TVL will give you much worse execution on a $10K buy than a $5M TVL pool.

Slippage impactExit risk indicator

LP concentration

What percentage of pool liquidity is owned by the top 1–3 liquidity providers. If one wallet owns 80%+ of the liquidity, they can remove it instantly — causing a price collapse. This is the mechanism behind most rugpulls.

Rugpull signalConcentration risk

Volume/liquidity ratio

A ratio of 24h volume to total pool liquidity. Ratios above 5–10× are unusual and often indicate wash trading — artificial volume generated to make a low-quality token appear active and attract buyers.

Wash trading signalManipulation indicator

Liquidity lock status

Whether the LP tokens are locked in a time-lock contract (e.g. Unicrypt, Team.Finance). Locked liquidity reduces — but does not eliminate — rugpull risk. Always verify the lock expiry date.

Lock verificationTime-lock expiry

DEX Guru Due Diligence Workflow: Step-by-Step Token Research Process

A systematic approach to token research using DEX Guru significantly reduces the chance of trading into scams, low-quality projects, or manipulated markets.

  1. Search by contract address — not by name or ticker. Many scam tokens impersonate legitimate projects with similar names. The contract address is the only unambiguous identifier.
  2. Check liquidity depth and LP concentration — is there enough liquidity to exit your intended position without severe slippage? Is liquidity concentrated in 1–2 wallets?
  3. Review volume-to-liquidity ratio — ratios above 5–10× warrant extra scepticism. Cross-reference on Etherscan or equivalent to verify transaction legitimacy.
  4. Examine the transaction history — are buys and sells balanced? Are there large wallet dumps interspersed with retail buys? Unusual patterns are red flags.
  5. Look up top trader wallets — when did they enter? Are they still holding or distributing? Track 2–3 of the largest recent buyers to assess their behaviour pattern.
  6. Cross-reference with a contract auditor — DEX Guru shows trading data, not contract code quality. Use Honeypot.is or a similar tool to verify the contract isn't a honeypot (token that can be bought but not sold).
  7. Verify socials and team independently — DEX Guru data is on-chain. Off-chain legitimacy (team identity, GitHub activity, audit reports) must be verified separately.

Best Practices for Using DEX Guru Effectively and Safely

  • Always search by contract address, not token name — DEX Guru's search will surface the correct contract, avoiding impersonator tokens with identical names.
  • Treat DEX Guru as one layer of research — complement it with block explorers (Etherscan), contract auditors (Honeypot.is, Token Sniffer), and project socials. No single tool is sufficient.
  • Evaluate liquidity before sizing a trade — check whether the pool has enough depth to absorb your position without major slippage, and whether you can exit at a reasonable price.
  • Cross-check volume quality — high volume on low liquidity is almost always wash trading. Verify transaction patterns on the chain explorer before trusting volume-based signals.
  • Use wallet tracking for context, not as copy-trading — smart money signals are informational, not directional. Following a wallet blindly without understanding the position is a common losing strategy.
  • Verify DEX Guru data against the chain explorer — for critical due diligence, confirm key figures (LP ownership, recent large transactions) independently on Etherscan or the relevant chain explorer.
  • Set realistic expectations for data freshness — DEX Guru updates continuously but may lag a few seconds to minutes during high chain congestion. For time-critical trades, use the chain's own block explorer for the most current state.

Troubleshooting DEX Guru: Missing Tokens, Stale Data, and Wrong Chain Issues

"The token I'm looking for doesn't appear in DEX Guru"

  • Search using the exact contract address rather than the token name — newly launched or low-volume tokens may not appear in name-based search results.
  • Verify the token's chain is currently supported by DEX Guru. Tokens on chains not yet indexed will not appear regardless of how you search.
  • Very new tokens (launched within the last few minutes to hours) may take a short time to be indexed after their first trades are confirmed on-chain.

"The price on DEX Guru looks different from what I see on the DEX"

  • DEX Guru aggregates data from multiple pools — if a token trades on several pools with different prices, you may see blended or pool-specific data depending on the view selected.
  • Select the specific pool you're trading on to see that pool's exact current price rather than an aggregate view.
  • During periods of extreme volatility or chain congestion, DEX Guru's data may lag slightly. Always confirm the live price directly in the DEX interface before executing a trade.

"Wallet analytics show less history than I expected"

  • DEX Guru indexes only DEX trades — direct transfers, CEX withdrawals, and non-DEX contract interactions will not appear in the wallet history.
  • History depth may vary by chain — some chains have longer indexed history than others on the platform.
Ground truth is always on-chain: DEX Guru is an analytics layer over blockchain data. If anything looks incorrect, verify directly on the relevant chain's block explorer — that is always the definitive source of truth.
